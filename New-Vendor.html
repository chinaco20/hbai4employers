The actual title on the report fomr is "Hawaii Carpenters Joint Trust Funds Employers Monthly Report To Trustees". Around here we just call it the Monthly Report, so if you say that we, that is what we will. The (ER) is symbolic for Employ(ER) as opposed to Employ(EE) contributions, and is fairly universal for fringes and benefits.

For now we are only able to offer spreadsheet forms for the Master Agreements, which are the IU and DL rate codes. This is a work in progress and we expect to offer all rate codes as spreadsheet forms eventually.

What is a layout?

Each layout has a specific target employer, based on their roster size and payroll system. You could also think of a layout as a template of sorts, they are all based on the same formulas and functions in the background. Each spreadsheet form file name begins with an underscore and then a letter code that indicates the layout used for that rate. You can refer to the table below on their meaning as well as the criteria for when they can be the most effective. Having several layouts was a way to accommodate many different roster sizes and payroll methods accross the base of employers. Why the funny prepend? It is mostly for us to be able to keep the forms organized when we alphabetize the files in a folder.

Cheat Sheets

Cheat sheets show rates as we have them loaded in our accounting system and in the spreadsheet forms that we make available yearly. They contain a full rate grid for all apprentice classes, a summarizing note of any changes from the previous fringe rates and an itemized list of all the rates that changed and what the previous agreement rate was. If we have supporting documentation from the empowered organization or association, that will be attached as well.

Penny Variances

Penny variances between the total due in our accounting system and the employer payment were ingrained in our spreadsheet forms. The inaccuracy was shorting members by a penny in certain situations causing employers to make adjustments to agree with our spreadsheet total while we were in turn would make equal and opposite adjustments to credit the member accounts. If you are seeing these variances, we have a report form that will correct this. If you would like to see when these penny variances occur click the More button.

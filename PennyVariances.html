Penny Variances

Penny variances occur when an over simplified spreadsheet tries to emulate a payroll system. Before 2019 our spreadsheet aggregated hours accross an entire apprentice class then applied the fund rate. A payroll system will asses the liability to each member first and then aggregate the individual amounts. Agreggating hours before applying the rate allows for lost rounding events. The penny variances conditions are very specific:

  1. The fund rate is an odd number.
  2. At least 2 members in the same classification have partial hours of 1/2 hour or more.
Under these conditions, for every 2 members there will be one lost rounding event equivalent to one penny. I know the penny seems petty (it is), but we do not have an accounting bucket to take that penny from. Every penny is accounted for in our system, and besides, it is satisfying to balance to the penny. Since 2019, all of the spreadsheet forms except for the _g layout avoid the penny variances. If you are seeing these variances and would like for them to stop please switch to a current spreadsheet form.
